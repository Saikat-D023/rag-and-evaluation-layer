Retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) is a technique that combines a language model with an external knowledge source. Instead of answering from its parameters alone, the model first retrieves relevant documents or chunks from a database or index. Those retrieved snippets are then appended to the user’s question and passed into the model as additional context. Because the model is “grounded” in these retrieved facts, it can give more accurate, up-to-date, and verifiable answers. This approach is especially useful when you need an AI assistant to work over private data, large document collections, or constantly changing information.```
